# Product

Related: [[README]] | [[../docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-19-pods-cycle-i-mvp-design|Cycle I MVP design]]

## Register

product

## Users

Pods serves people joining or running small accountability communities inside Nimiq Pay. Participants need to understand their current obligation, membership state, financial exposure, and next action without learning the internal state machine. Creators need to form public or private Pods, review applications, and follow participation without gaining hidden financial or verification powers.

## Product Purpose

Pods turns recurring real-world activity into a clear, NIM-backed commitment lifecycle. A successful interface always answers three questions: what state am I in, what happens next, and what does that mean for my committed NIM? The Cycle I product must demonstrate this lifecycle end to end with real wallets, frozen terms, centralized Pods-team verification, and deterministic settlement behavior.

## Brand Personality

Earned, assured, and kinetic. Pods should feel premium and motivating without resembling a casino, trading terminal, or generic fitness tracker. Motion confirms progress and state changes; it never delays the task.

## Anti-references

- Moonwalk imitation or a fitness-only product frame.
- Crypto casino visuals, neon-on-black dashboards, speculative language, or fake urgency.
- Generic navy-and-gold fintech luxury.
- Repetitive card grids, decorative glass, gradient text, and motion without state meaning.
- Interfaces that expose internal enum names or contradict the same participant state across routes.

## Design Principles

1. One participant state, one consistent meaning everywhere.
2. Lead with the next real action, then show supporting context.
3. Financial state must be explicit, persistent, and refresh-safe.
4. Public discovery creates momentum, but never overrides an existing relationship with a Pod.
5. Earned progress should feel alive through restrained, purposeful motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Use semantic controls, visible focus states, minimum 44 by 44 pixel touch targets, text labels alongside color-coded states, and reduced-motion fallbacks. Financial and verification outcomes must remain understandable without color, animation, or prior crypto knowledge.
