# Pods Phase 4 Mobile UX Audit

## Verdict

The current build fails the product-trust bar. It has a strong visual voice on Today and honest underlying privacy rules, but the experience still exposes its route structure, status model, and control inventory directly to users. The result feels assembled rather than directed.

This is not primarily a color, image, or animation problem. It is an information architecture and interaction hierarchy problem. Another cosmetic pass would preserve the same confusion.

## What Works

1. Today is the clearest expression of the product. It answers what needs attention and leads with one action.
2. Public and private Pod boundaries are enforced correctly in the data layer. Discover only returns public Pods. Private Pods remain invitation-only.
3. The proof model already distinguishes reviewer-only evidence from Pod-shared evidence, and messaging has durable delivery, retry, reaction, and read-state foundations.

## Priority Issues

### P0: The proof screen states the wrong financial consequence

The current contract is `full_refund_alpha`, and the frozen rules say activity review never changes the return amount. The proof screen nevertheless shows `At risk 0.1 NIM`. This is not harmless motivational copy. It contradicts the financial contract at the exact moment a participant submits evidence.

Impact: users can believe poor activity performance may cost principal when the alpha guarantees full return. This blocks a trustworthy release.

Correction: derive all occurrence consequence copy from `settlementMode`. For the alpha, show `Activity slice 0.1 NIM` and `Your full principal remains returnable`. Reserve `At risk` for a later proportional settlement mode that can actually forfeit value.

Suggested command: `$impeccable clarify the occurrence financial consequence for full_refund_alpha`

### P1: The Pod information architecture exposes five routes instead of one experience

Room, Today, Activity, Members, and Contract are five equal persistent tabs. They duplicate global Today, My Pods, and Messages, and force users to understand the app's routing model. This directly contradicts the locked design instruction that a chat room should prefer a back button and one context control.

Impact: participants cannot form a simple mental model of what a Pod is. The same Pod appears as a lifecycle card, a room, an activity surface, and a local Today surface.

Correction: make the Pod room the authenticated Pod home. Use a compact header with back, Pod identity, a notification control, and one context control. Put proofs, members, contract, share, and creator settings into that context surface. Place a slim current-occurrence strip below the header with the deadline, state, and one action. Keep Today global only.

Suggested command: `$impeccable distill the Pod route into a chat-first accountability room`

### P1: Messaging ignores familiar mobile conversation behavior

Every message permanently renders Reply and four reactions. Creator messages may add Pin or Hide. The Pod composer floats 82 pixels above the safe-area bottom even though the Pod room does not render global navigation. Direct-message controls are reduced to 34 pixels, below the product's 44 pixel target.

Impact: each message looks like an admin record, the timeline becomes visually noisy, and the primary composer wastes vertical space.

Correction: show only reactions that already exist as compact badges. Open React, Reply, Pin, Hide, and Copy through long press and an accessible More button. Hide the global bottom navigation on conversation routes. Fix the composer to the safe-area bottom like a native chat. Preserve keyboard focus with an explicit focus ring.

Suggested command: `$impeccable normalize Pod rooms and DMs to native mobile chat behavior`

### P1: Proof submission is organized around implementation steps, not participant confidence

The user must first save a text draft, then expose an attachment studio, then interact with raw file controls, then select visibility, save again, and finally submit. The screen combines financial consequence, commitment, summary, artifact, attachment type, native inputs, privacy, immutable-choice warning, save state, and final submission.

Impact: the most trust-sensitive flow has the highest cognitive load. Users cannot answer who sees each item in two seconds, and Save versus Submit is ambiguous.

Correction: open one proof composer from the Pod room. Keep summary and required public artifact at the top. Add evidence through a single action. After each attachment, require one clear visibility choice: `Reviewer only` or `Share with this Pod`. Show the choice on the attachment tile. Allow draft persistence automatically. Finish with one sticky `Review and submit` action and a compact confirmation sheet.

Suggested command: `$impeccable focus the proof flow around evidence visibility and one final action`

### P1: The global shell creates duplicate destinations and heavy chrome

My Pods correctly represents lifecycle and financial state. Messages currently repeats the same Pods under Pod Rooms, while Discover adds three large segments plus a template filter. The header always says PODS and then repeats the route title below it. The 72 pixel bottom navigation floats above the edge and can cover card actions.

Impact: the app spends too much screen space explaining where the user is while still leaving destination boundaries unclear.

Correction: make My Pods the single inventory of owned and joined Pods. Let its cards open the chat-first room. Make Messages about people, DMs, and requests only. Replace the wordmark with the current route title on utility routes. Keep the bottom bar flush to the safe area at roughly 60 to 64 pixels and remove the full-cell active pill. In Discover, use one compact Pods, People, Following switcher and a single context-aware card action.

Suggested command: `$impeccable distill the global shell and remove duplicate Pod destinations`

## Persona Red Flags

- Distracted mobile participant: must scan five message controls per entry and loses usable space to floating navigation and composer gaps.
- First-time applicant: sees `Open to apply` as a status beside `View Pod`, without learning that Discover is public-only and private Pods arrive through invitations.
- Privacy-conscious participant: cannot understand proof visibility quickly because the choice appears late inside the attachment workflow.
- Creator: cannot distinguish the Pod as a community room from its separate status, activity, member, and contract routes.
- Financially cautious participant: receives false `At risk` language despite a guaranteed full-return alpha contract.

## Minor Findings

- Profile presets directly reuse the same activity photographs used by Pod covers. Human identity needs a separate portrait, initials, or generated-avatar system.
- The same small media pool repeats across unrelated Pods, which weakens community authenticity.
- The Discover card already has a useful information control, but the separate status band and full action duplicate intent. Use one relationship-aware action.
- `Open to apply` should become `Accepting applications` only when a status label is genuinely useful. The primary action should simply be `Apply`, `Application pending`, `Fund`, `Open room`, or `Manage` based on the viewer's relationship.
- `1 people moving together` needs singular grammar.
- The profile settings sheet needs `aria-modal`, initial focus, focus trapping, Escape handling, and focus restoration.
- Two overlapping CSS systems make fixed-bar spacing, legacy colors, and responsive overrides difficult to reason about.
- The black N bubble in browser captures is the Next.js development indicator and is not product UI.

## Decisions to Confirm

1. Recommended: My Pods remains the only Pod inventory, and Messages becomes People plus Requests. Alternative: keep Pod Rooms inside Messages as a secondary, non-default filter.
2. Recommended: the Pod context control opens one sheet containing Proofs, Members, Contract, Share, and creator controls. Alternative: expose Members separately and place the rest in the context sheet.
